Description

The family Entelodotidae are related to the ancestors of modern pigs. However these weren't like your ordinary pigs that sit around and do nothing. There is a reason why their nicknames are 'Hell pigs' or 'Terminator pigs'. It had a bite force of upto 2500 pounds per square inch and it was a vicious bone crushing omnivore. It's aggressive behavior could be compared to that of a bear on steroids chasing a deer, which is also on steroids. Entelodonts had full sets of teeth, including large canines, heavy incisors, pointed premolars, and relatively simple flat molars. These features might have been used for defense or offense. In larger species, a bison-like spinal hump supported the weight of the heavy head. The length and proportions of their leg bones are consistent with other hoofed animals that run well on open ground. Like many artiodactyls, they had cloven hooves, with two toes touching the ground, and the remaining two being vestigial. Some entolodonts had bony protrusions on their skulls, which they used to beat the crap out of any unlucky bastard that crossed paths with them. The extent to which entelodonts were predators is debated, however, wear patterns on entelodont teeth do resemble modern carnivorans in some respects, and the later and larger species have binocular vision. There is also evidence of entelodonts caching the bodies of early camelids as a food source, as well as multiple examples of toothmarks on bones.
